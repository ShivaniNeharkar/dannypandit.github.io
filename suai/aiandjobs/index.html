Are SU Students Worried About AI Impacting Their Job Security?
Students from Syracuse University are consciously thinking more about the future of AI and the way it will affect their job prospects.

By Za'Tozia Duffie, Seth Quinn, and Nada Merghani

Published : 27th July 2024

Students at Syracuse University are no strangers to the growth of AI. Students across majors and departments have usually worked with AI in some capacity. However, this familiarity with AI does not necessary quell fears that graduates may eventually have to compete with robots for full-time, high-paying jobs.

In Figure 1, you can see a breakdown of which students feel the most anxiety about AI as they enter the job market.

Img 1: Scene from South Park where they complained about their jobs being taken

An article released by CBS news showed that the growth of AI was responsible for the loss of over 4,000 jobs in May 2023. Students at Syracuse University are seriously considering job loss as a posibility--to the point where they are changing their careers over it.

In figure 2, you can see how many students across majors shifted their career paths due to the growth of AI.

Overall, the data shows that students across majors and with varying degrees of technical knowledge are thinking about AI and the way its growth will affect their future job prospects.
